A collection of ideas and concepts that I like.

Test Sizes

How to think about the different types of tests that we can create, and how the most useful dimension is “size”.

Chesterton’s Fence

Do not remove a fence until you know why it was put up in the first place.

Conway’s Law

Organizations which design systems (in the broad sense used here) are constrained to produce designs which are copies of the communication structures of these organizations.

Inverse Conway Manoeuvre

My answer to Conway’s Law.

The Inverse Conway Manoeuvre: Influence communication structures through thoughtful architecture that deliberately seeks to reform collaboration and communication between contributors for improved adaptiveness.

Law of Conservation of Complexity

Years ago I thought that I had come up with this idea. However, I recently discovered that others had beat me to it and had already described it in writing. It is a reference to existing physical laws such as the Law of Conservation of {Mass,Energy}.

Every application has an inherent amount of complexity that cannot be removed or hidden. Instead, it must be dealt with, either in product development or in user interaction.

Example takeaway from this: when you simplify a workflow for a user, but retain all business requirements, the complexity does not go away. It must be moved from the user, into the application. As a consequence, we also take on the additional cost of that complexity because the user is no longer doing the work for us.
