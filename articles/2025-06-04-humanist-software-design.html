Humanist Software Design

2025-06-04 Robert Pitts

Humanist software design means designing software that communicates a mental model from its author to its readers and users—from human to human. Controlling machines is secondary. It’s about expressing behavior in a way that’s understandable and intuitive to another person.

This principle applies at every level, from graphical interfaces to low level tools. In all cases, we reduce cognitive load by organizing complexity; expressing cogent, focused ideas on a need-to-know basis. We do this by layering and structuring interfaces so that only what's relevant is exposed at the right time, supporting clear and unburdened thought.

Let's make software with a thoughtful, human-centered approach to conveying intent. Correctness, velocity, teamwork, trust... The rest will all fall into place.
